CORROSION
Metallic Corrosion
The metals are extracted from their metallic compounds (ores). During the extraction, ores are reduced to their metallic state by applying energy in the form of various processes.
In the pure metallic state, the metals are unstable as they are considered to be in an excited state (higher energy state).
Definition of Corrosion
The process of slow destruction of metal due to chemical or electrochemical attack of the environment at their surface, resulting in the formation of compounds such as oxides, carbonates, sulphides etc., is called corrosion.
Examples
Rust is hydrated ferric oxide → Fe₂O₃·xH₂O
Tarnishing of silver
Basic Reaction
Metal → Metallic Compound + Energy
(corrosion / oxidation)
